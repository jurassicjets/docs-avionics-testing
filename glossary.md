# Glossary

Acronyms, part-number conventions, and terms-of-art that come up in avionics docs. Add entries as they come up — every term that confused you on first encounter belongs here.

## Instruments and LRUs

- **LRU** — Line Replaceable Unit. An avionics box designed to be swapped at the line (i.e., on the ramp) without shop tooling.
- **ADI** — Attitude Director Indicator. The artificial horizon with flight-director command bars.
- **HSI** — Horizontal Situation Indicator. Heading display with course/glideslope information.
- **RMI** — Radio Magnetic Indicator. Compass card with VOR/ADF bearing pointers.
- **ASI** — Airspeed Indicator.
- **ALT** — Altimeter.
- **VSI** — Vertical Speed Indicator.
- **ADF** — Automatic Direction Finder.
- **DME** — Distance Measuring Equipment.
- **VOR** — VHF Omnidirectional Range.
- **XPDR** — Transponder.
- **FMC** — Flight Management Computer.
- **IRU** / **IRS** — Inertial Reference Unit / System.

## Buses and signals

- **ARINC 429** — One-way serial digital bus, 32-bit words, 12.5 kHz or 100 kHz.
- **ARINC 561** — Earlier 6-wire serial standard, used in early INS installations.
- **Synchro** — 3-wire AC position-transducer signal (often 26 VAC, 400 Hz reference).
- **Resolver** — 4-wire AC position-transducer signal (sin/cos pair).

## Electrical

- **28 VDC** — Standard aircraft DC bus voltage.
- **115 VAC / 400 Hz** — Standard aircraft AC bus.
- **26 VAC / 400 Hz** — Reference voltage for synchros and resolvers.

## Documents

- **CMM** — Component Maintenance Manual. Manufacturer's shop-level service document.
- **IPC** — Illustrated Parts Catalog. Aircraft-level parts and assemblies reference.
- **WDM** — Wiring Diagram Manual. Aircraft-level wiring reference.
